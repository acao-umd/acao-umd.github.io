Projects


Bracket

This is the custom language I've been working on in my spare time. It started off parodying the real programming language Racket, but soon became its own things after I realized I had the power to implement whatever kind of features I wanted. The compiler (and interpreter) are written completely in C, and currently only supports x86-64.

Twitter Bots

To conduct research on Twitter bots, I worked with a group of other students and we bought our own 300 or so bots to observe their behavior and monitor their statistics. We did this over several months, and saw several trends in the data gathered from usernames, follower counts, and others.

Markov Text Generator

When researching Markov Chains and their applications, I made a text generator in Python (with minimal natural language processing) using the stochastic nature of Markov Chains. While it was nothing close to the generative AI and chat bots we see today, the text generator was able to output (somewhat coherent) sentences when fed sufficient training data (like the Bee Movie script).
